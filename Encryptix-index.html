Protecting Patient Data in Veterinary Practices

Encryptix is a student project developed by third-year IT and Cybersecurity students at MTU. Our goal is to help local veterinary clinics understand how encryption protects client and patient data. We focus on clear explanations and hands-on demonstrations that make cybersecurity approachable and practical.

What we offer

About the Project

Encryptix was created by Bradley, Sean, Vitalina, and Eoghan as part of their IT3 Group Project, supervised by Hristo Trifonov. The project aims to raise awareness about the importance of data protection in local veterinary clinics and show how encryption can secure medical records, communications, and client information.

The website highlights four main encryption concepts: symmetric, asymmetric, hashing, and hybrid encryption. Each one is demonstrated in a simple, visual format that helps visitors understand the basics of how encryption works and why it matters for clinics of all sizes.

Who We Aim to Help

We focus on helping independent veterinary clinics that manage client and patient data daily, multi-location practices that need secure record transfers, and animal hospitals that rely on safe communication with referral partners. The project also supports veterinary staff and students who want to understand how encryption fits into real-world clinical workflows.

Interactive Encryption Demonstrations

Our Services page features four encryption demos that represent key concepts in modern cybersecurity. Each one shows how data can be encrypted, decrypted, or hashed to ensure information stays private and tamper-proof.

Disclaimer: These demos are for educational use only. They are simplified visualisations that demonstrate encryption principles but are not suitable for securing real data.
