Asymmetric Encryption Demo (Public and Private Keys)

This demo shows how a message can be locked with a public key and unlocked with a private key. Imagine a vet clinic shares their public key so partner shelters can send information securely, while the clinic keeps the private key secret.

Behind the scenes this uses RSA-OAEP with SHA-256 in your browser. This demo is meant to be a simple repersentation of the TLS handshake done by programs such as OpenSSL. Asymmetric encryption is vital in encrypting internet traffic such as emails as without encrypting a message it leaves its contents vunerable to packet sniffing programs such as wireshark
